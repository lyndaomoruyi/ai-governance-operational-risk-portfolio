Project Overview

From scattered AI adoption to compliance-ready control visibility

This project models a current banking compliance problem: AI tools can be adopted across business units before compliance teams have full visibility into data exposure, vendor risk, human oversight, missing evidence, and ongoing monitoring responsibilities.

Business Problem

AI use cases can expand across departments without one clear inventory, evidence trail, owner, review cadence, or control monitoring view.

Compliance Solution

The workflow tracks AI use cases, vendor review status, data sensitivity, human oversight, missing evidence, risk tier, issues, and remediation actions.

Business Value

Compliance, risk, IT security, vendor management, and audit teams gain a practical view of approval status, open issues, and evidence readiness.

Compliance Monitoring Workflow

How the system makes AI risk review usable

The workflow is built around compliance operations: intake, risk rating, vendor evidence, control mapping, remediation tracking, and human validation.

Inventory AI Use

Capture tool, use case, business area, platform type, data sensitivity, customer impact, and owner.

Review Risk

Classify risk based on data exposure, customer impact, approval status, oversight, and evidence gaps.

Validate Vendor

Track due diligence, SOC reports, model cards, subcontractor review, privacy terms, and contract review.

Map Controls

Connect each risk area to control objective, evidence expectation, control owner, and monitoring frequency.

Track Issues

Document remediation owner, due date, issue status, escalation need, and human-reviewed next action.

Compliance & Governance

Built for banking compliance, IT risk, and audit readiness

The project uses AI as a monitored workflow layer, not an unchecked decision-maker. The workbook emphasizes human review, owner accountability, control evidence, and explainable risk status.

AI Use Case Inventory

Tracks who is using AI, why, where the tool is hosted, whether customer data is involved, and who owns review.

Third-Party Risk

Surfaces vendor due diligence gaps, subcontractor review, retention terms, contract/privacy review, and required follow-up.

Control Monitoring

Maps AI governance risks to control objectives, evidence, owner, frequency, and issue remediation.
