Executive Summary

This project converts a predictable inventory dashboard idea into a risk, compliance, evidence, and decision-support case study.

It demonstrates business analysis, workflow design, compliance thinking, operational risk visibility, UAT validation, and responsible AI use.

What I Built

  • An intake-to-reporting workflow for operational risk and client compliance review.
  • A synthetic data model that protects confidentiality while demonstrating realistic monitoring logic.
  • A workbook with dashboard views, risk classification, control mapping, and UAT-ready outputs.
  • An evidence package with BRD, user stories, UAT test cases, governance notes, and sources.

Production Context

Built from real operational visibility work

This public portfolio version uses synthetic data, but the business problem is grounded in real operational experience supporting collectible medallion coin production, supply, inventory visibility, replenishment coordination, client reporting, and account follow-up across major entertainment client environments.

Business impact behind the case study

The real work helped improve visibility into medallion coin availability, reduce the risk of running out of guest-facing products, and support better coordination across high-visibility entertainment account environments.

Portfolio expansion

The command center expands that operational foundation into an employer-facing AI-assisted workflow using synthetic data, risk logic, control evidence, UAT validation, and stakeholder-ready reporting.

The Problem

Why manual tracking breaks down

The risk is not only low inventory. The risk is delayed visibility when operational signals are scattered across trackers, emails, documentation follow-ups, and client reporting obligations.

Separate trackersInventory, vendor, client, and documentation items live in different places.
Delayed visibilityHigh-risk items are harder to identify early.
Unclear ownershipFollow-up actions can sit without a clean owner/status view.
Missing evidenceDocumentation and control gaps become reporting issues.
Late escalationIssues may not surface until they affect service or client readiness.
Reporting pressureStakeholders need clean summaries and account-level visibility.

Before / After Business Value

The transformation the command center demonstrates

Before: Fragmented Review

  • Inventory, documentation, vendor follow-up, and reporting status can sit in separate trackers or messages.
  • Stockout risk and overdue reporting may not surface until follow-up becomes urgent.
  • Managers may lack one view showing risk, owner, action, evidence, and status.

After: Structured Risk Visibility

  • Risk signals are grouped into one monitoring workflow with account-level views.
  • High-risk items, documentation gaps, and escalations are surfaced for action.
  • Controls, UAT validation, and evidence notes make the work reviewable for stakeholders.
Risk VisibilityEarlier attention to exposure

Highlights replenishment exposure, overdue reporting, and documentation gaps before they are buried in manual follow-up.

Client ReadinessCleaner account-level views
