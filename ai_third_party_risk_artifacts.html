Project Overview

Problem, solution, and evidence

The project demonstrates how a banking compliance team could monitor AI use cases, vendor risk, missing evidence, human oversight, control ownership, and remediation activity using synthetic data.

Business Problem

AI tools can be introduced across business areas before compliance, risk, IT security, vendor management, and audit teams have one clear view of business owner, data sensitivity, customer impact, vendor evidence, review status, and open issues.

Compliance Solution

The solution creates a structured AI use-case inventory and monitoring workflow that tracks risk tier, approval status, vendor due diligence, evidence gaps, control mapping, issue ownership, and review cadence.

Portfolio Evidence

The workbook includes dashboard views, AI inventory, vendor review, control matrix, issue log, UAT, sources, and data dictionary. This page presents the supporting artifacts in a readable format.

Authenticity Note

This is an independent portfolio project inspired by regulated banking compliance experience. It uses synthetic data only and does not claim that the workflow was used by Commerce Bank or any employer.

Business Requirements Document

Business need and functional requirements

Financial institutions need a practical way to identify, review, monitor, and evidence AI use cases across departments.

Stakeholders

  • Compliance
  • Risk Management
  • IT Security
  • Vendor Management
  • Business Owners
  • Internal Audit
  • Legal / Privacy

Functional Requirements

  1. Capture each AI use case with business area, owner, vendor/tool, data sensitivity, customer impact, and platform type.
  2. Assign risk score and risk tier based on data sensitivity, customer impact, review status, oversight level, and approval status.
  3. Track missing evidence such as SOC reports, model cards, data-flow diagrams, vendor due diligence, and human oversight attestations.
  4. Track issues with owner, due date, severity, status, and remediation action.
  5. Map AI governance risks to controls, evidence expectations, owner, and review frequency.
  6. Provide dashboard-level reporting for high-risk use cases, missing evidence, overdue reviews, and open issues.
  7. Document UAT scenarios to validate risk logic, evidence tracking, and compliance monitoring outputs.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Synthetic data only.
  • Human review required for AI-assisted risk outputs.
  • Clear evidence trail for audit readiness.
  • Simple workbook-based prototype that can be implemented in Excel, SharePoint, Power BI, Jira, ServiceNow, Archer, OneTrust, or similar systems.

Success Measures

  • AI use cases are visible in one inventory.
  • High-risk and overdue reviews are easy to identify.
  • Missing evidence is clearly tracked.
  • Issues have owners, due dates, status, and remediation actions.
  • Controls are mapped to evidence expectations and review cadence.

AI Governance Validation Note

Responsible AI boundaries

This project treats AI as a decision-support layer, not as an autonomous decision-maker.

Human Review Principle

AI-assisted outputs should support compliance review by summarizing risk, highlighting missing evidence, and recommending next actions. Final approval, hold, escalation, or remediation decisions remain with accountable human reviewers.

Data Protection

This portfolio uses synthetic data only. It does not include confidential banking records, customer information, employer data, vendor contracts, internal procedures, or regulatory submissions.

Risk Boundaries

  • Designed to support AI use-case inventory.
  • Designed to support third-party/vendor AI review.
  • Designed to support evidence tracking and control monitoring.
  • Not designed to make credit decisions, file regulatory reports, or approve AI use without human review.

Governance Alignment

  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework.
  • Third-party risk management life-cycle principles.
  • Model risk concepts around governance, validation, monitoring, and controls.
  • Compliance documentation and evidence management practices.
