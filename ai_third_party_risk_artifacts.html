Business Requirements Document

Business Need

Financial institutions need a practical way to identify, review, monitor, and evidence AI use cases across departments before data exposure, vendor risk, missing oversight, or undocumented AI usage creates compliance risk.

Stakeholders

Compliance, risk management, IT security, vendor management, business owners, internal audit, legal, and privacy teams need one shared view of AI use, review status, risk tier, and evidence readiness.

Functional Requirements

  • Capture each AI use case with business area, owner, vendor/tool, platform type, data sensitivity, customer impact, and human oversight status.
  • Assign risk tier based on data sensitivity, customer impact, approval status, review status, oversight level, and evidence gaps.
  • Track vendor evidence such as SOC reports, model cards, data-flow diagrams, due diligence status, contract/privacy review, and subcontractor review.
  • Map AI governance risks to control objectives, evidence expectations, control owner, frequency, and testing status.
  • Track issues, remediation owner, due date, severity, status, and recommended next action.

UAT Test Cases

UAT-001: Restricted Data Risk Rating

When a use case includes restricted data and customer-impacting workflow, the risk tier should calculate as High or Critical.

UAT-002: Missing Evidence Gap

When required evidence is missing, the dashboard should reflect the missing evidence count and generate a review gap.

UAT-003: Overdue Review

When the next review date is past due, the overdue review count should increase and the item should require follow-up.

UAT-004: Human Oversight Gap

When oversight is limited for a customer-impacting use case, the workflow should flag a control gap and increase risk priority.

UAT-005: Vendor Due Diligence

Vendor-hosted AI use cases should require due diligence evidence, privacy/contract review, and documented vendor ownership.

UAT-006: Closed Issues

When an issue is closed, it should be excluded from the open issue count and remain available as audit evidence.

AI Governance Validation Note

Human Review Principle

AI-assisted outputs support review by summarizing risk, highlighting evidence gaps, and recommending next actions. Final approval, hold, escalation, or remediation decisions remain with accountable human reviewers.

Data Protection Boundary

This portfolio uses synthetic data only. It does not include confidential banking records, customer information, employer data, vendor contracts, internal procedures, or regulatory submissions.

Risk Boundary

The workflow supports AI use-case inventory, vendor review, evidence tracking, compliance control monitoring, issue remediation, and audit-ready documentation. It is not designed to make credit decisions, replace compliance officers, or approve AI use without human review.

Governance Alignment

The project is aligned conceptually with AI risk management, third-party risk management life-cycle principles, model risk governance concepts, and compliance documentation practices.

Sources and Research Basis

This project direction is grounded in current compliance and governance themes affecting financial services and regulated organizations:

  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework for AI governance, mapping, measurement, and management concepts.
  • Interagency third-party risk management guidance for vendor due diligence and ongoing monitoring.
  • Model risk management concepts for governance, validation, monitoring, controls, and vendor-provided tools.
  • Financial-services compliance concerns around AI use, human oversight, data governance, and evidence readiness.
