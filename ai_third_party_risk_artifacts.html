Dataset & Workbook Structure

Synthetic AI Use-Case Dataset

The dataset models 30 AI use cases across banking functions such as lending, branch operations, fraud operations, compliance, call center operations, internal audit, and vendor management. It is synthetic and contains no real customer, employer, vendor, or banking records.

Risk Fields Tracked

Fields include AI tool/vendor, business unit, platform type, model type, data sensitivity, customer impact, human oversight, approval status, review status, evidence completeness, next review date, risk score, risk tier, control owner, and AI review note.

Workbook Tabs

The workbook includes a dashboard, AI use-case inventory, vendor risk review, control matrix, issue/action log, UAT tracker, sources, and data dictionary.

Why It Matters

The workbook gives compliance, risk, IT security, vendor management, and audit teams a practical view of AI risk status, evidence readiness, missing reviews, and open remediation items.

Business Requirements Document

Business Need

Financial institutions need a practical way to identify, review, monitor, and evidence AI use cases across departments before undocumented AI usage, customer-data exposure, vendor risk, or missing oversight creates compliance risk.

Stakeholders

Compliance, risk management, IT security, vendor management, business owners, internal audit, legal, and privacy teams need one shared view of AI use, approval status, risk tier, owner, and evidence readiness.

Functional Requirements

  • Capture each AI use case with owner, tool, business area, platform type, data sensitivity, and customer impact.
  • Assign risk tier based on approval status, review status, oversight level, data sensitivity, and evidence gaps.
  • Track evidence such as SOC reports, model cards, data-flow diagrams, due diligence, and contract/privacy review.
  • Map AI governance risks to controls, evidence expectations, control owner, monitoring frequency, and testing status.
  • Track issue owner, due date, severity, remediation action, and status.

Success Measures

  • High-risk AI use cases are visible in one inventory.
  • Missing evidence and overdue review items are easy to identify.
  • Open issues have owners and due dates.
  • Control ownership and evidence expectations are documented.

UAT Test Cases

UAT-001: Restricted Data Risk Rating

When a use case includes restricted data and customer-impacting workflow, the risk tier should calculate as High or Critical.

UAT-002: Missing Evidence Gap

When SOC report, model card, or required evidence is missing, the dashboard should reflect the missing evidence count and create a review gap.

UAT-003: Overdue Review

When the next review date is past due, the overdue review count should increase and the item should require follow-up.

UAT-004: Human Oversight Gap

When human oversight is limited for a customer-impacting use case, the workflow should flag a control gap and increase risk priority.

UAT-005: Vendor Due Diligence

Vendor-hosted AI use cases should require due diligence evidence, privacy/contract review, subcontractor review, and documented vendor ownership.

UAT-006: Closed Issues

When an issue is closed, it should be excluded from the open issue count while remaining available for audit evidence.

AI Governance Validation Note

Human Review Principle

AI-assisted outputs support compliance review by summarizing risk, highlighting missing evidence, and recommending next actions. Final approval, hold, escalation, or remediation decisions remain with accountable human reviewers.

Data Protection Boundary

This portfolio uses synthetic data only. It does not include confidential banking records, customer information, employer data, vendor contracts, internal procedures, or regulatory submissions.

Risk Boundary

The workflow supports AI use-case inventory, vendor review, evidence tracking, control monitoring, issue remediation, and audit-ready documentation. It is not designed to make credit decisions, replace compliance officers, or approve AI use without human review.

Governance Alignment

The project is aligned conceptually with AI risk management, third-party risk management life-cycle principles, model risk governance concepts, and compliance documentation practices.

Sources and Research Basis

This project direction is grounded in current compliance and governance themes affecting financial services and regulated organizations:

  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework for AI governance, mapping, measurement, and management concepts.
  • Interagency third-party risk management guidance for vendor due diligence and ongoing monitoring.
  • Model risk management concepts for governance, validation, monitoring, controls, and vendor-provided tools.
  • Financial-services compliance concerns around AI use, human oversight, data governance, and evidence readiness.
