# AI Governance Validation Note

## Purpose
This project demonstrates responsible AI governance for a synthetic banking compliance workflow. AI is treated as a decision-support layer, not as an autonomous decision-maker.

## Human Review Principle
AI-assisted outputs should support compliance review by summarizing risk, highlighting missing evidence, and recommending next actions. Final approval, hold, escalation, or remediation decisions remain with accountable human reviewers.

## Data Protection
This portfolio uses synthetic data only. It does not include confidential banking records, customer information, employer data, vendor contracts, internal procedures, or regulatory submissions.

## Risk Boundaries
The workflow is designed to support:
- AI use-case inventory
- third-party/vendor AI review
- evidence tracking
- compliance control monitoring
- issue remediation tracking
- audit-ready documentation

It is not designed to make credit decisions, replace compliance officers, file regulatory reports, or approve AI use without human review.

## Governance Alignment
The project is aligned conceptually with:
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- Third-party risk management life-cycle principles
- Model risk management concepts around governance, validation, monitoring, and controls
- Compliance documentation and evidence management practices
