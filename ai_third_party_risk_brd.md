# Business Requirements Document

## Project Name
AI Use Case Inventory, Vendor Risk & Compliance Control Monitoring System

## Business Need
Financial institutions need a practical way to identify, review, monitor, and evidence AI use cases across departments. Compliance teams must be able to see which AI tools are being used, whether vendors have been reviewed, what data is accessed, whether human oversight exists, and which items require remediation.

## Stakeholders
- Compliance
- Risk Management
- IT Security
- Vendor Management
- Business Owners
- Internal Audit
- Legal / Privacy

## Functional Requirements
1. Capture each AI use case with business area, owner, vendor/tool, data sensitivity, customer impact, and platform type.
2. Assign a risk score and risk tier based on data sensitivity, customer impact, review status, oversight level, and approval status.
3. Track missing evidence such as SOC reports, model cards, data-flow diagrams, vendor due diligence, and human oversight attestations.
4. Track issues with owner, due date, severity, status, and remediation action.
5. Map AI governance risks to controls, evidence expectations, owner, and review frequency.
6. Provide dashboard-level reporting for high-risk use cases, missing evidence, overdue reviews, and open issues.
7. Document UAT scenarios to validate risk logic, evidence tracking, and compliance monitoring outputs.

## Non-Functional Requirements
- Synthetic data only
- Human review required for AI-assisted risk outputs
- Clear evidence trail for audit readiness
- Simple workbook-based prototype that can be implemented in Excel, SharePoint, Power BI, Jira, ServiceNow, Archer, OneTrust, or similar systems

## Success Measures
- AI use cases are visible in one inventory
- High-risk and overdue reviews are easy to identify
- Missing evidence is tracked with owners and due dates
- Controls are mapped to evidence and monitoring cadence
- Compliance reviewers can explain and validate outputs
