Business Analysis Artifact

Business Requirements Document

Project: AI-Assisted Operational Risk & Client Compliance Command Center

Objective: Create an AI-assisted workflow that helps operational and compliance teams identify replenishment risk, documentation gaps, client reporting issues, and overdue follow-up actions before they become service or compliance concerns.

Stakeholders

Commercial OperationsProject / Business AnalystCompliance / Risk OwnerVendor ManagementFulfillment / InventoryClient-facing Stakeholders

Functional Requirements

  • Ingest synthetic inventory and operational tracking data.
  • Calculate days of cover and compare inventory to reorder thresholds.
  • Classify each record by risk type and risk rating.
  • Flag missing documentation, overdue vendor follow-ups, and pending client reports.
  • Generate recommended action items and escalation notes.
  • Maintain owner, due date, status, and evidence fields.
  • Provide dashboard views for leadership and stakeholder reporting.
  • Support UAT validation through pass/fail test cases.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Use synthetic data in the public portfolio version.
  • Keep risk logic explainable and reviewable.
  • Maintain human validation checkpoints.
  • Provide downloadable artifacts for recruiter and employer review.
  • Publish the project through an employer-facing portfolio website.

Agile / Scrum Artifact

User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

Story 1: Inventory Risk Identification

As an operations stakeholder, I want the system to flag low inventory and short days-of-cover records so replenishment actions can begin before a stockout occurs.

  • Records below reorder threshold are flagged.
  • Records with low days of cover receive elevated risk.
  • Recommended action includes replenishment review.

Story 2: Client Compliance Visibility

As a compliance stakeholder, I want documentation and client reporting gaps visible so required follow-up is tracked and evidence is maintained.

  • Missing documentation is classified as a control gap.
  • Pending or overdue client reports are flagged.
  • Owner and due date fields are populated.

Story 3: AI-Generated Action Items

As a project analyst, I want AI-generated recommended actions so manual follow-up drafting is reduced.

  • Each risk record has a recommended next action.
  • High-risk records include escalation language.
  • Actions are reviewable before use.

Story 4: Leadership Reporting

As a manager, I want summarized risk views so I can quickly understand open issues, high-risk accounts, and overdue actions.

  • Dashboard shows high, medium, and low risk counts.
  • Dashboard highlights overdue actions.
  • Executive summary language can be generated from risk trends.

AI Governance Artifact

AI Governance & Human Validation Note

Purpose: This portfolio demonstrates responsible AI use in a business-analysis and compliance context. AI assists with classification, summarization, action generation, and documentation support; final review remains human-owned.

Governance Controls

  • Synthetic data only; no confidential business data is used.
  • Human review is required before any AI-generated summary, risk rating, or action item is treated as final.
  • AI outputs are checked against defined business rules: inventory threshold, days of cover, documentation status, follow-up aging, and client reporting status.
  • High-risk records require evidence review and escalation confirmation.
  • Risk ratings remain explainable through visible input fields and scoring logic.

Validation Approach

  • Compare AI risk classification to business rules.
  • Check whether recommended actions match the risk type.
  • Confirm that high-risk records include clear follow-up ownership.
  • Validate that client reporting and documentation gaps are not ignored.
  • Retain source data and evidence fields for audit readiness.
Responsible AI Positioning: This project treats AI as a productivity and decision-support tool, not a replacement for business judgment, compliance review, or management approval.
